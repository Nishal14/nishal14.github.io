About Me

Hi, I’m Nishal Thomas, a Class 11 student passionate about machine learning, neural networks, and interpretability research. I build research-oriented projects that explore how models learn structure, generalize, and process information.

My current work spans mechanistic interpretability, generalization dynamics like grokking, and compact transformer models for algorithmic reasoning. I value reproducibility and clarity, and I enjoy turning conceptual ideas into documented, open-source research pipelines.

Beyond project work, I write technical explainers and guides that make complex topics more accessible. I’m particularly interested in the intersection of AI, mathematics, and structured reasoning, and I’m looking to grow further in research and experimentation.

I study at St. Mary’s Sr. Sec. School in New Delhi, India. You’ll find links to my projects, code, and writings throughout this site — feel free to explore!
