FormInv: A Measurement Protocol for Semantic Invariance in Mathematical Reasoning Benchmarks

Nishal Thomas (First Author)

ICML 2026 AI4Math Workshop (Poster)

Introduces FormInv, a benchmark and evaluation framework for measuring semantic invariance in mathematical reasoning systems. Developed a dataset of Lean4-grounded theorem paraphrases spanning 8 transformation families and evaluated robustness across 9 frontier language models. Proposed Semantic Consistency Rate (SCR) and cross-model unanimity auditing, revealing formulation-sensitive failure modes and benchmark construction errors hidden by aggregate accuracy metrics.

When Models Know Without Representing: Behavioural–Representational Dissociation in LLM Abstraction Hierarchies

Workshop Submission (Under Review)

Constructed 50 abstraction ladders spanning nine levels of conceptual abstraction across mathematics and scientific domains. Investigated behavioural–representational dissociation in language models using representation geometry, abstraction coherence metrics, and activation patching, across model scales from 70M to 70B parameters, to study abstraction formation and scaling behaviour.
